“Hands in the Soil: The Legacy of Maya Farming in Belize Today”

Overview

Agriculture has been a long cornerstone for Belizean life, going back thousands of years to the time of the ancient Maya. In an age without modern tools, the Maya shaped the natural world with deep ecological wisdom, transforming forest and wetlands into agricultural landscapes. Their innovations were to raise fields, canal systems and seasonal crop rotations by sustained cities like Caracol, Lamanai and Nim Li Punit. (Planet Archaeology, n.d; Atlas Obscura, n.d.)

Rationale Behind

Recognize the sustainable practices of ancient maya agriculture Analyze the impact of colonialism on land use and labour systems
